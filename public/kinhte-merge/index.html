Saigon - Ho Chi Minh City has developed a prosperous agricultural,
forestry, and fishery region, producing an abundance of food and other agricultural products. The growth of agriculture has driven the development of towns, markets, seaports, to meet the needs of goods processing, transport, and consumption, as well as the development of such industries as shipbuilding, milling, and mechanical processing. Currently, the city's economy is undergoing a structural shift, integrating agricultural development with industry, commerce, and services, promoting the growth of multi-sector industries and high-tech industries, both in breadth and depth,
to ensure its role as the economic nucleus, of the region and nationwide.

Ho Chi Minh City has experienced rapid growth in its trade and service sectors,
transforming it into a major commercial hub with a wide range of domestic
and international goods. The city has also become a significant international
trade and tourism center. Its market-oriented economy has witnessed rapid
growth across manufacture, commerce, and services, making it a leading
financial center for distribution and import-export activities, serving as the
economic engine of the Southern key economic zone.
